Present general, concise information on worker-cooperative business structure. VII. Provide founding members with contact info for cooperative development organizations such as the Cooperative Development Institute the Democracy at Work Institute, and the U.S. Federation of Worker Cooperatives (https://www.usworker.coop/startups/). VIII. Provide decision help with choosing legal business entity form, typically either LLC or cooperative corporation (IRS form 1120-C). Cooperative corporation should be the default choice if worker-owners can anticipate making at least minimum wage from the start, as this is required by law since they are considered employees. If they cannot expect to make at least minimum wage from the beginning, they may want to form under an LLC with a transition plan in place to switch to a cooperative corporation as soon as expected revenues are sufficient. The reason is that cooperative corporations as a business form are typically more resilient against possible efforts to “de-cooperativize” them in the future. IX. Provide functionality to help choose business name (checking to see if name is already taken, advising that the business can only have “cooperative” in the name in CA if it is incorporated specifically as a cooperative corporation) (possibly use https://www.uspto.gov/trademark and state Secretary of State registry website). X. Generate business charter based on business type and name chosen; any forms that need to be signed generated and presented to founding members via DocuSign. (https://www.co-oplaw.org/ is highly useful) XI. Help members schedule founding meeting, and provide pre-generated agenda with necessary business items, especially the selection of Board of Directors. XII. Provide info for non-managerial members to immediately contact and organize with United Electrical, Radio, and Machine Workers of America (UE) (https://www.ueunion.org/) (one of two unions of which I am aware that have initiatives to organize union-coops, the other being the United Steelworkers; the UE is organized along a model more in line with the democratic worker cooperative itself, however).
