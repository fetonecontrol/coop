Legal

  • Cooperative corporation should be the default choice if worker-owners can anticipate making at least minimum wage from the start, as this is required by law since they are considered employees. If they cannot expect to make at least minimum wage from the beginning, they may want to form under an LLC with a transition plan in place to switch to a cooperative corporation as soon as expected revenues are sufficient. The reason is that cooperative corporations as a business form are typically more resilient against possible efforts to “de-cooperativize” them in the future.
  • Choose business name (checking to see if name is already taken). Also check your respective State Gov. website. USPTO.
  • To generate a business charter, create forms via DocuSign
  • Get your members registered with the organize with United Electrical, Radio, and Machine Workers of America UE.

  • To file your taxes as a "Cooperative Association": IRS-CO-OP-TAX(Business formation costs are tax deductible and up to $5,000 can be deducted in the first year on your business tax return.
